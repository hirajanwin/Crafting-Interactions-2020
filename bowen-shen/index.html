Social Interactions
for Elderly

Overview

The elderly have social needs, just as people of all other ages. However, they sometimes lack means of socialising.

This project tries to solve this problem with the concept of an interactive community bulletin board that allows the elderly to learn about and sign up for nearby community gatherings.

TYPE

Individual Project

Timespan

15 weeks, 2020

Role

Research, interaction design, prototyping

Explore the problem space

The elderly's needs for social activities

Needs for being social, and lack of chances

Elderly people have needs for social interactions, but they (especially the retired ones) have fewer chances to interact with others as young people do, due to the lack of access to social environments such as schools and offices.

Damage of loneliness

60 years old and older who reported feeling lonely saw a 45% increase in their risk of death

Isolated survey respondents also had a 59% greater risk of mental and physical decline than their more social counterparts

*Combatting the Epidemic of Loneliness in Seniors

How elderly adults currently socialize

Since this project's main focus is not on user research, I did simple research into how older people socialise by asking my family about my grandparents' behaviours.

I learnt that there are two main ways how elderly people socialise:

existing social circle

Elderly mostly interact with people around where they live, or people who they knew before.

Hobbies

Elderly who have specific hobbies are more likely to make new friends.

Problem statement

HMW...?

How might we help elderly socialise,
and find interests that they will have fun spending time on?

Ideation

Interactive Community Bulletin Board

Initial concept sketches

The basic concept I had was to create an interactive community board that enables the elderly to learn about and sign up for community gatherings with easy interactions. Once one is signed up, a reminder will be send to him/her before the event.

In the initial version, the context was imagined to be a futuristic retirement community.

The interaction for signup is to tap one's finger on the gathering posting on the screen. The device will recognise the user's identity immediately by fingerprint, so that the event is signed up under their name without extra steps for logging in. A reminder will be sent to smart devices in the room, in case the elder person does not check their phone often.

Wireframes & UX Flow

In the wireframe version, I decided to make the context more realistic. The community board is imagined to be situated in a common suburb, in an area where there's exposure to more residents, like in a community centre. The fingerprint recognition tech is also replaced with RFID technology, which will require a resident ID card. The confirmations and reminders will be sent to the user's phone.
