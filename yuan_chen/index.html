Overview

HearNote is a phone app helping seniors who have hearing loss communicate with others in person. Notifications and scripts make conversations smoother and efficient.

Background Challenge

The aging population in the US is large, growing, with a lot of influence socially and economically. People are live longer; nearly 80% of the population now lives past the age of 65. Approximately one in three people between the ages of 65 and 74 has hearing loss, and nearly half of those older than 75 has difficulty hearing. But, some people may not want to admit they have trouble hearing.

Structure & Wireframes

Feature One: Detection

When the user activates the app, it start to listen to the user and the person who is talking to him/her. The app will transcribe every sentence. The app will also detect if the conversation is too loud that might disturb other people. If the sound is too loud, the screen will turn red.

Feature Two: Adjusting

The user can try to enlarge the font size if needed. By tapping the “Aa” button, and drag the bar to adjust the font size.

User Testing

ROUND ONE

A/B Testing

I used the social media platform to figure out the most efficient way to adjust text size. Although dragging looks cool, clicking a button seems to be more acceptable.
FUN FACT: People who chose "Drag" are most likely my designer friends, while others prefer "Click," who are more likely the potential users.

ROUND TWO

Feedback from classmates

The word “loud” is not necessary. Red color is enough to indicate that the person who is speaking is too loud.
How can I exit?
How can I edit the text if the app hear  it wrong?
When talking to an app, people will condense their sentence.  It is not necessary to transcribe when  the elderly is speaking.

ROUND THREE

Suggestion from instructor

Might need to test the prototype with the elderly. Not sure if the plus and minus button is large enough for them.
The “x” button looks more like one for retrieving a message, not for exit the app.
The actual button for retrieve a message does not look like one for this purpose.
Can I record the message?
The interactions need to be as simple as possible. Limited to only “tap” trigger. It’s hard for elderly to learn triggers like drag / hold and release / etc.

ROUND FOUR

Requests from elderly

“Tap-only”
They don’t want to hold the button while talking.
They actually want to store the conversations.
Stored conversations needs to be classified by time/people, so that they can help recall the memories.
