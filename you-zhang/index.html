Bring the music to people who cannot hear.

ASL Music is a digital product concept that uses vibrations and visual contents to bring musical experience to people with hearing disabilities.

The Process

Normally the full design process includes discovery, diagnosis, design, and deploy. However, due to the limited time of this project, we skipped some parts and focused on the prototyping part.

00 Context

There are approximately 466 million people world wide who have disabling hearing loss but also want to experience and enjoy music as well. How might we deliver musical experiences to people with hearing disabilities through other sense?

01 Discovery

Research

Although people who have disabling hearing loss have never actually 'heard' music, their bodies and ears are able to pick up different sound waves, vibration and low notes. Dr. Dean Shibata, a professor in the Department of Radiology at the University of Washington, published his music-related findings. "The Brain is incredibly adaptable. In someone who is deaf, the young brain takes advantage of valuable real estate in the brain by processing vibrations in the parts of the brain that would otherwise be used to process sound," Shibata shares.

In addition to the use of vibration, the ASL (American Sign Language) music "movement" is another approach to harmoniously bridge a hearing-centric experience over to a visually-centric experience. “I do at least forty to fifty events per year, and that includes festivals which may include four shows a day,” says Kelly Kurdi, a multilingual interpreter with Amber G. Productions who studied at Gallaudet University, an institution for the deaf. Amber suggests that solely signing a song’s words and, then, pausing during instrumentals is an injustice. 02 Diagnosis

Opportunity, Guiding Policy, User Journey

According to the research, we've found the opportunity to create a product that uses vibrations and visual contents to bring musical experience to people with disabling hearing disabilities, which is based on these guiding policies:

Intuitive

To provide intuitive and user-friendly interaction

Engaging

To deliver engaging content and delightful experience 

Encouraging

To create a community that encourages more ASL contents
