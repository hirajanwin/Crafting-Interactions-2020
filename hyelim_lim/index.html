Overview

The service consists of two parts:
Smart flatware and an application.

Smart flatware enables people to detect allergens right before eating food. On the application, not only can people track their eating history, but also they can share it with their doctors. The app also helps people to know information about food ingredients when they are grocery shopping or searching for restaurants.

Problem Context : Prevalence

Food allergy is prevalent in America

As food allergy affects 1 in 10 adults, 1 in 13 children in the US, food allergy becomes one of the major health concerns among people.

Problem Context : Severeness

Over half of people who have food allergy have experienced severe symptoms

Food allergy is like a soundless gun. Sometimes allergy reactions to food can be severe, and life-threatening. Over 200,000 people visit the emergency room every year.

Problem Context : Market aspects

The allergy diagnostic and therapeutic market size is growing every year

Rapid urbanization, pollution, poor dietary habits increases the incidences of allergy conditions across the globe. While 50 million american are affected by allergies every year, there is no effective cure for food allergy in sight.

Challenge

How might we help people who have food allergic asthma so that it makes their lives more safe?

"It's like you're sending your kids out into a world full of loaded guns.
And it really is like that — the wrong bite can kill them, it's that level of anxiety.”
- Halley Gilbert has two daughters with life-threatening food allergies.

Secondary Research

Competitor Analysis

I chose three major allergy detector companies to evaluate their services and discover opportunity areas. Based on my insights, I created seven parameters: Ease-of-use, The number of allergens that can be detected, portability, price, allergy information support, accuracy, and time. 

The competitor analysis produced the following insights.

Opportunity area: Ease-of-use, allergy information support
To use most detectors, people should insert a piece of food into the device and wait for a while. In terms of requiring extra work, it is not an intuitive way to detect allergens.

Service requirement: Portability
The size of competitive products is small and handy because people should bring a device everywhere.

Technical&business issues: The number of allergens, time, accuracy, price
‍There are four technical factors that I cannot determine. Though these issues are beyond UX design, time and the number of allergens are relevant to usability. I decided to consider the two factors, focusing more on opportunity areas and service requirements in this project. 

Discovering pain points

User Journey

To map the user journey. I set up three major situations where people could use the service: Eating home-cook meal, grocery shopping, finding and eating at restaurants

Depending on the situation people face, they will have different goals, actions, and touchpoints.
On the other hand, the flow of the feeling is affected by their symptoms.

Onboarding: Questionnaire
